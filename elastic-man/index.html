The Physics Engine Behind Elastic Man

What makes Elastic Man special is its sophisticated soft-body physics simulation. Unlike simple sprite stretching, Elastic Man uses:

Vertex Deformation

The face consists of thousands of connected vertices that respond to force. When you pull one area, surrounding vertices move naturally, creating realistic skin stretching rather than simple distortion.

Tension Simulation

Different facial areas have varying resistance. The cheeks stretch easily, while areas around the eyes and mouth have more structural tension, mimicking real human anatomy.

Rebound Physics

Release the mouse, and the face doesn't snap back instantly. Instead, it rebounds with realistic oscillation, gradually settling back to its original position—just like real skin.
