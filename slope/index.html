Advanced Strategies For Slope

Want to achieve epic scores? These Slope tips will extend your runs:

Look Ahead, Not At Your Ball

Focus your eyes on the distant slope, not directly on your ball. Your peripheral vision tracks the ball's position, while your central vision spots upcoming obstacles. This gives you maximum reaction time to plan your path .

Make Small, Precise Movements

Large, jerky movements often overcorrect and lead to edges or obstacles. Use small, controlled adjustments to stay centered. Gentle taps rather than held movements give you finer control .

Learn Obstacle Patterns

While procedurally generated, obstacles appear in recognizable patterns. You'll start recognizing common formations—diagonal lines, checkerboards, narrow corridors. Pattern recognition speeds up your reactions .

Stay Centered When Possible

The center of the path gives you maximum room to maneuver left or right. Edge-hugging limits your options when sudden obstacles appear. Return to center after navigating each challenge .

Don't Watch Your Score

Glancing at your score breaks concentration and often leads to immediate crashes. Trust that you're doing well and focus entirely on the path ahead. Check your score only after you crash .
