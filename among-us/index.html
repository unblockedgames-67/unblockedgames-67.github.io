How To Be A Better Crewmate

Want to catch impostors consistently? These Among Us tips will improve your Crewmate game:

Stick Together

Safety in numbers. Impostors are less likely to kill when multiple players are present. Pair up with other crewmates, especially when traveling to distant parts of the map. If you see someone alone, consider following them—you might witness a kill or deter one.

Remember Task Locations

Learn where tasks are located on each map. If you see someone standing at a non-task location for suspiciously long, they might be faking. Similarly, if you witness someone completing a visual task like asteroids or shields, they're confirmed crewmate.

Pay Attention To Meetings

Listen carefully during discussions. Players who are overly defensive, change their story, or accuse without evidence often raise red flags. Take notes on who accuses whom and who defends suspicious players. Vote patterns reveal impostors over multiple rounds.

Use Admin And Security

Learn to use map-specific tools like Admin table (shows player locations) and Security cameras (The Skeld) or Vitals (Polus). These tools provide valuable information about who's where and who might have died.

How To Be A Better Impostor

Want to deceive everyone? These Among Us impostor strategies will help:

Create Alibis

Always have a story. If asked where you were, mention a task you were supposedly doing. Better yet, fake tasks convincingly—stand at task locations for realistic durations and move away as if you completed something.

Kill Strategically, Not Randomly

Don't kill immediately or randomly. Isolate targets away from witnesses. Kill near vents for quick escapes. Sometimes it's better to wait for the perfect opportunity than to rush and get caught.

Sabotage Wisely

Use sabotages to create chaos and split up groups. Oxygen depletion and reactor meltdown force players to separate to fix them—perfect killing opportunities. Lights sabotage reduces vision, making kills harder to witness.

Blend In During Meetings

Don't be too quiet or too loud. Participate in discussions, offer reasonable suspicions, and vote with the group when it makes sense. Overly aggressive accusations draw suspicion.
