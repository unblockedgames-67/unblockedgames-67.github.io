Training Systems Explained

Your duck has four core stats, each improved through a unique mini-game:

Running Training

A side-scrolling obstacle course where your duck must jump over hurdles. Press spacebar to jump at the right moment. Successfully clearing hurdles increases running stat. Higher running speed helps in land races.

Swimming Training

Navigate your duck through underwater currents while avoiding obstacles. Use arrow keys to dodge and weave. Swimming stat affects performance in water races and determines how fast your duck moves through pools.

Flying Training

Flap your wings to stay airborne while collecting clouds. Click or press spacebar repeatedly to flap. The longer you stay up, the more flying experience you gain. Flying stat is crucial for air races.

Climbing Training

Scale vertical walls by jumping between ledges. Time your jumps carefully to avoid falling. Climbing stat affects races with elevation changes and obstacle courses.

Advanced Strategies For Duck Life

Want to become World Champion faster? These Duck Life tips will accelerate your progress:

Early Game Focus

Don't try to balance everything immediately. Pick one or two skills and max them first. Winning early tournaments provides money to train other stats faster.

Food Management

Your duck needs food to train effectively. Buy the most expensive food you can afford—it provides more training energy per dollar than cheap options.

Training Efficiency

Each training mini-game has optimal strategies:

  • Running - Jump slightly before obstacles to clear them cleanly
  • Swimming - Stay in the center lane for most maneuverability
  • Flying - Steady rhythm beats frantic flapping
  • Climbing - Look ahead to plan your jump sequence

Stat Prioritization

Different tournaments favor different skills. Scout opponents before entering to see which races appear most frequently in each tournament.
